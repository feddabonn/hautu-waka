Hautū Waka

Navigating Complexity

A Māori wayfinding framework for situations where conditions shift faster than plans can keep up.

What it's for

Some situations can be analysed and solved. Others resist that approach — policy design where stakeholders shift positions, organisational change where each adaptation changes the terrain, any environment where what worked last month stops working. These aren't problems to solve. They're conditions to find your way through.

How it works

The diagram shows six stages/states. You can move through them as a linear design process, and that works. But the real power is in treating them as states you move between as conditions require — sometimes progressing, sometimes cycling back, sometimes finding that your destination changes mid-journey. Polynesian navigators didn't use fixed maps because the ocean doesn't hold still. This framework applies that principle to complex contemporary work.

Using this diagram

Click any stage/state to explore it. The arrows show possible movements, not prescribed sequences.

Based on work by Roimata Taniwha-Pao'o and Ayla Hoeta, with guidance from Matua Rereata Makiha.

Developed at The Southern Initiative and Auckland Co-Design Lab.

    Tools

    Processes and methods that help navigate each stage. Click a muscle name to see what it means.

    Emotional Culture Deck

    Teams use emotion cards to surface how they want to feel at work, how they don't want to feel, and what behaviours create those feelings. Makes emotional culture visible and discussable.

    Used in: Whakariterite Te Rāpunga Te Whāinga Te Whiwhinga
    Develops: Emotional Awareness And Processing Relational Intelligence Collective Sense Making Recognition And Reciprocity Mindfulness And Presence

    More Than Words (Imagery Cards)

    Visual prompts help people access thoughts, feelings, memories they struggle to articulate verbally. Used in facilitation, counselling, reflection work.

    Used in: Te Rāpunga Te Kitenga
    Develops: Emotional Awareness And Processing Multiple Ways Of Knowing Mindfulness And Presence Sensory Perception Comfort With Mystery And Uncertainty Purpose And Orientation

    6 Conditions of Systems Change

    FSG framework for analysing systems across six conditions — three explicit (policies, practices, resource flows) and three implicit (relationships, power dynamics, mental models). Used to diagnose where change efforts should focus.

    Used in: Te Kitenga
    Develops: Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Relational Intelligence Environmental Awareness And Immersion Multiple Ways Of Knowing Cyclic And Non Linear Thinking Collective Sense Making

    Te Whare Tapa Whā

    Framework for assessing holistic wellbeing — checking in across Tinana, Wairua, Whānau, Hinengaro, and Whenua. Can be individual or collective reflection.

    Used in: Whakariterite Te Rāpunga Te Whiwhinga
    Develops: Body Awareness And Somatic Intelligence Emotional Awareness And Processing Relational Intelligence Purpose And Orientation Rootedness And Belonging Multiple Ways Of Knowing Mindfulness And Presence

    Theory of Change

    Maps causal pathways from activities to outcomes (If, By, Then, & Then, & Eventually). Forces articulation of assumptions about how change happens.

    Used in: Whakariterite Te Whāinga
    Develops: Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Purpose And Orientation Collective Sense Making Cyclic And Non Linear Thinking Comfort With Mystery And Uncertainty

    Deeper Questions

    45 question cards designed to move conversations past surface level. Prompts storytelling and personal disclosure.

    Used in: Te Rāpunga Te Rāwenga
    Develops: Relational Intelligence Recognition And Reciprocity Emotional Awareness And Processing Mindfulness And Presence Comfort With Mystery And Uncertainty Multiple Ways Of Knowing

    6 Thinking Hats

    De Bono's parallel thinking method. Group adopts one "hat" (mode of thinking) at a time — White (facts), Red (feelings), Black (caution), Yellow (benefits), Green (creativity), Blue (process).

    Used in: Te Kitenga Te Whāinga
    Develops: Multiple Ways Of Knowing Collective Sense Making Emotional Awareness And Processing Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Discipline Of Not Acting Productive Friction

    DVFE

    Decision evaluation lens assessing options against four criteria — Desirability (Do people want it?), Viability (Can it sustain itself?), Feasibility (Can we actually do it?), Ethicality (Should we do it?).

    Used in: Te Whāinga
    Develops: Multiple Ways Of Knowing Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Collective Sense Making Purpose And Orientation Productive Friction Restraint As Embodied Practice

    Reflexiv Model

    Reflective cycle — Experience → What (stood out) → So What (patterns, implications) → Now What (actions) → Experiment → new Experience. Derived from Kolb and Driscoll.

    Used in: Te Kitenga Te Whiwhinga Te Rāwenga
    Develops: Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Cyclic And Non Linear Thinking Continuous Adaptation With Place Mindfulness And Presence

    Project Funeral / Closing Ritual

    Structured practice for marking endings — acknowledging what's complete, what's being left behind, what was learned.

    Used in: Te Rāwenga
    Develops: Working With Endings Grief And Release In Collective Contexts Compost And Return Collective Sense Making

    Pixar Story Spine

    Narrative structure template — "Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that... Until finally... And ever since then..." Used to construct coherent narratives from messy experience.

    Used in: Te Rāwenga
    Develops: Multiple Ways Of Knowing Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Purpose And Orientation Collective Sense Making

    PESTLE

    Environmental scanning framework — Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental factors. Used to read the broader context you're operating in.

    Used in: Whakariterite Te Kitenga
    Develops: Environmental Awareness And Immersion Continuous Adaptation With Place Pattern Recognition And Triangulation Multiple Ways Of Knowing Collective Sense Making

    Two Loops Model

    Visual framework showing two curves — one system in decline, another emerging. Identifies roles: pioneers of the new, hospice workers for the old, connectors between them.

    Used in: Whakariterite Te Rāwenga
    Develops: Compost And Return Working With Endings Temporal Submission Cyclic And Non Linear Thinking Pattern Recognition And Triangulation

    Muscles

    The muscles are collective capabilities developed through practicing Hautū Waka. They're organised using Te Whare Tapa Whā — the Māori wellbeing model — as a structure. Like a house with four walls and a foundation, all must be strong for the structure to stand.

    Whenua (Foundation)

    Whenua is the foundation upon which the whare stands. Without this grounding, the four walls cannot hold.

    Rootedness and Belonging

    Connection to place shapes identity. Knowing where you come from helps you navigate where you're going.

    Developed by: Te Whare Tapa Whā

    Relationship with Environment

    Environment isn't backdrop — it's relation. Reciprocal relationship with places and spaces you inhabit.

    Developed by: No specific tools mapped

    Environmental Awareness and Immersion

    Being involved in the world rather than abstracting from it. Environment communicates constantly.

    Developed by: 6 Conditions of Systems Change PESTLE

    Continuous Adaptation with Place

    Place changes. You change. Staying connected means refreshing understanding continuously.

    Developed by: Reflexiv Model PESTLE

    Temporal Submission

    Place has its own rhythms. Growing happens according to the logic of context, not urgency of the grower.

    Developed by: Two Loops Model

    Compost and Return

    What dies enables growth to continue. Old patterns, identities, certainties — their dissolution creates conditions for what comes next.

    Developed by: Project Funeral / Closing Ritual Two Loops Model

    Taha Tinana (Physical)

    The physical dimension — body awareness, sensory perception, and embodied practice.

    Body Awareness and Somatic Intelligence

    Your body registers information before your conscious mind does. Tension, ease, gut responses are data.

    Developed by: Te Whare Tapa Whā

    Sensory Perception

    Intelligence comes through all senses, not just sight.

    Developed by: More Than Words (Imagery Cards)

    Limits and Rest

    Bodies have limits. Exhaustion isn't an obstacle to overcome but information about what's sustainable.

    Developed by: No specific tools mapped

    Restraint as Embodied Practice

    Not-doing takes practice. The body learns to hold back, pause before acting.

    Developed by: DVFE

    Taha Wairua (Spiritual)

    The spiritual dimension — connection to purpose, ancestry, mystery, and endings.

    Connection to Ancestral Knowledge

    Tūpuna had been doing this for ages. Traditional practices contain wisdom tested over generations.

    Developed by: No specific tools mapped

    Purpose and Orientation

    Work connects to something larger than immediate tasks. Purpose provides orientation when conditions shift.

    Developed by: More Than Words (Imagery Cards) Te Whare Tapa Whā Theory of Change DVFE Pixar Story Spine

    Comfort with Mystery and Uncertainty

    Not everything can be known in advance. Emergence is real. Certainty is often illusion.

    Developed by: More Than Words (Imagery Cards) Theory of Change Deeper Questions

    Working with Endings

    Everything ends. Spiritual maturity includes orienting toward mortality without rancour.

    Developed by: Project Funeral / Closing Ritual Two Loops Model

    Acceptance of the Uncontrollable

    Some things can't be changed by effort. Finding equanimity with what must be accepted.

    Developed by: No specific tools mapped

    Taha Whānau (Social)

    The social dimension — relationships, collective process, recognition, and shared grief.

    Relational Intelligence

    Relationships aren't just context for work — they are the work. Quality of relationships determines what becomes possible.

    Developed by: Emotional Culture Deck 6 Conditions of Systems Change Te Whare Tapa Whā Deeper Questions

    Collective Sense-Making

    Understanding emerges through collective process, not individual genius.

    Developed by: Emotional Culture Deck 6 Conditions of Systems Change Theory of Change 6 Thinking Hats DVFE Project Funeral / Closing Ritual Pixar Story Spine PESTLE

    Recognition and Reciprocity

    Seeing and acknowledging others' mana and potential. Relationships characterised by mutual respect.

    Developed by: Emotional Culture Deck Deeper Questions

    Productive Friction

    Honest collective work involves disagreement, tension, struggle. Conflict isn't failure of relationship.

    Developed by: 6 Thinking Hats DVFE

    Grief and Release in Collective Contexts

    Collectives lose members. Collective grief — acknowledging loss together — is how groups remain capable of forming new attachments.

    Developed by: Project Funeral / Closing Ritual

    Taha Hinengaro (Mental/Emotional)

    The mental and emotional dimension — presence, pattern recognition, multiple intelligences, and the discipline of waiting.

    Multiple Ways of Knowing

    Rational analysis is one tool among many. Intuition, embodied knowing, emotional intelligence — all valid.

    Cyclic and Non-Linear Thinking

    Process isn't always forward progression. Cycling back is part of navigation.

    Developed by: 6 Conditions of Systems Change Theory of Change Reflexiv Model Two Loops Model

    Tolerance for Frustration

    Innovation is struggle. Frustration isn't sign of failure — it's the texture of the work.

    Developed by: No specific tools mapped

    Discipline of Not-Acting

    Sitting with incompletion takes mental discipline. Resisting premature closure.
